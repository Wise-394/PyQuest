[b]Tuple[/b]
[i]Some things are meant to stay the way they are.[/i]

[hr]

A [b]tuple[/b] is an ordered, [b]immutable[/b] collection of items in Python. Once a tuple is created, its contents cannot be changed. This makes tuples ideal for storing data that should remain constant throughout a program's execution.

While similar to lists, tuples are faster and signal to other developers that the data is not meant to be modified.

[b]In this level, you will:[/b]
[ul]
Create tuples using parentheses [b]()[/b]
Access tuple items using their [b]index[/b]
Understand the difference between a [b]tuple[/b] and a [b]list[/b]
[/ul]
