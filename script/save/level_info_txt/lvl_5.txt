[b]Comparison Operators[/b]
[i]To move forward, you must first learn to ask the right questions.[/i]

[hr]

[b]Comparison operators[/b] are used to evaluate the relationship between two values. Every comparison expression returns a [b]boolean[/b] result — either [b]True[/b] or [b]False[/b] — making them essential for controlling program flow.

These operators form the foundation of conditional logic, which you will build upon in later levels.

[b]In this level, you will:[/b]
[ul]
learn comparison operators: [b]== != > < >= <=[/b]
Compare numeric values, strings, and variables
Understand how comparison results integrate into decision-making logic
[/ul]
